What are the Hreflang tags?

The hraflang tags are small pieces of code that tell Google what language your website pages are written in, and what region they are for. Accordingly, these tags explicitly demonstrate to search engines, like Google and Yandex, what language version of your website to serve to the users searching in that specific language.

Сode example

link rel="alternate" href="http://example.com" hreflang="en-us" /> link rel="alternate" href="http://example.com" hreflang="en-au" /> link rel="alternate" href="http://example.com" hreflang="en-nz" />

Why do you need Hreflang tags on your website?

If you have more than one variation of your website with the same content in various languages, it is essential to have the hreflang tags on your website. So here are a few reasons why you should tell Google about your localized versions of the website.

  • SEO − to avoid duplicate content and make your multilingual web pages rank in SERP you should add the hreflang tags to your webpages. It allows Google to rank all pages of your website equally.
  • User experience − your website visitors will see content in their preferred language from the first click without having to switch the store view. It not only shows Google your website relevancy, but provides personalized experiences for every potential customer.
